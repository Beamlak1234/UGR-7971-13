R-L-C Circuit Analysis            

Theory


With reference to figure 1, the application of Kirchoff's law results, V=IR+jIXL+IjωC , Where, VR=IR,VL=jIXL,VC=IjωC V=I(R+jωL+1jωC) .......(1) Let Z be the net impedance of the circuit, this gives from equation(1), V=IZ=I(R+jωL+1jωC) Or, Z=R+j(ωL−1ωC)=(R2+(ωL−1ωC)2)∠tan−1⁡(ωL−1ωCR) ........ (2) Equation (2) gives the complex impedance(Z) which indicates that the circuit will become inductive if ωL>1ωC and then the sign of the angle of Z is positive. On the other hand, for ωL<1ωC , the circuit will become capacitive and the sign of the angle of Z is negative.
